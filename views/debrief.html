Understanding Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, like passwords. In this simulation, a deceptive login page mimicking the school's Microsoft login was created, prompting victims to enter their credentials, which may be used to gain access to their accounts.

Tips to Avoid Getting Phished

  1. Stay Informed: Keep up-to-date with the latest phishing techniques and attacks in your community
  2. Think Before You Click: Avoid clicking on links in suspicious emails or messages. Hover over links to verify their legitimacy.
  3. Verify Site Security: Check for "https" and a lock icon in the URL bar before submitting personal information. Also, check the URL for any spoofing.
  4. Regular Account Checks: Monitor your online accounts for any unauthorized activity.

Remember, there is no single way to be completely secure, but staying informed and vigilant is crucial for protecting you sensitive information online. Also, you are not alone; others have also fallen victim to this scam, so don't hesitate to report if you have been hacked.
