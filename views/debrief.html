Understanding Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, like passwords. In this simulation, a deceptive login page mimicking the school's Microsoft login was created, prompting victims to enter their credentials, which may be used to gain access to their accounts.

Tips to Avoid Phishing Scams

  1. Stay Informed: Keep up-to-date with the latest phishing techniques to recognize and avoid them effectively.
  2. Think Before You Click: Avoid clicking on links in suspicious emails or messages. Hover over links to verify their legitimacy.
  3. Use Anti-Phishing Tools: Install browser toolbars that identify and warn against phishing sites.
  4. Verify Site Security: Check for "https" and a lock icon in the URL bar before submitting personal information.
  5. Regular Account Checks: Monitor your online accounts regularly for any unauthorized activity.
  6. Keep Browser Updated: Update your web browser promptly to patch security vulnerabilities.
  7. Utilize Firewalls: Employ both desktop and network firewalls for enhanced protection against intruders.
  8. Beware of Pop-Ups: Block pop-ups and avoid clicking on suspicious ones, especially those with misleading cancel buttons.
  9. Avoid Sharing Personal Info: Never share sensitive information online unless you're certain about the recipient's legitimacy.
  10. Use Antivirus Software: Install and regularly update antivirus software to detect and prevent phishing attacks.

Remember, there is no single way to avoid all phishing attacks, but staying informed and vigilant is crucial for protecting yourself and your sensitive information online.
